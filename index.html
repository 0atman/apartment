Introduction

Tufte CSS is based on Tufte-LaTeX 11 See https://code.google.com/p/tufte-latex/ and http://rmarkdown.rstudio.com/tufte_handout_format.html. The goal is to define a style similar to that which Edward Tufte uses in his books and handouts. Tufte’s style is known for its extensive use of sidenotes, tight integration of graphics with text, and well-set typography.

Headings

Tufte CSS styles headings h1, h2, and h3.

In his later books2 2 http://www.edwardtufte.com/tufte/books_be, Tufte starts each section with a bit of vertical space, a non-indented paragraph, and sets the first few words of the sentence in small caps. To accomplish this using this style, use a span with class 'newthought', as demonstrated at the beginning of this paragraph.

Sidenotes

One of the most prominent and distinctive features of Tufte's style is the extensive use of sidenotes. There is a wide margin to provide ample room for sidenotes and small figures. Spans with class 'sidenote' inserted directly into the middle of p tags within each section put the sidenote at the correct height. 33 This is a sidenote.

Most sidenotes include footnote-style numberings, but you don't have to. This is a margin note. Notice there isn't a number preceding the note.These are called margin notes. They are currently identical to sidenotes but are kept semantically distinct because one might wish to style them separately.

Tables

Tabular data is presented with right-aligned text and minimal grid lines. Table labels are margin notes placed inside a p tag that wraps the table.
